Welcome to Elite Aviation’s Private Pilot Program!
You’ll find that the Syllabus guide below is a tool to guide you through our course. It provides a syllabus
overview, lesson objectives, homework assignments, resource list, and questions to answer or ask your
instructor.
For the best results through the course, we recommend following the guide as intended and coming in
prepared for each lesson. This includes completing the homework assignment and answering the study
questions on your own. The answer to each study question can be found in the homework or resource
material. If you find a particular question or topic to be difficult, don’t sweat it! That is why we have a
team of highly qualified Certified Flight Instructors to help you.
When you go over the material, keep in mind the four building blocks to successful learning.
• Rote: Rote knowledge is knowledge that is purely memorized. Often you do not need context
for this knowledge. You’ll experience Rote Knowledge most in the beginning of your training.
Such as learning of the speeds at which your aircraft operates.
• Understanding:Understanding occurs when you take the Rote Knowledge and apply context to
it. You’ll see this the most during the beginning of your training. An example of this is when you
understand the reason your aircraft has certain operating speeds for different maneuvers.
• Application: Application occurs when you take both Rote and Understanding and use it in a realworld example. Such as when you memorize the steps of a maneuver, understand what occurs
aerodynamically during that maneuver, and then be able to perform that maneuver
successfully. You’ll experience this level of learning throughout your training.
• Correlation: Correlation is the highest level of learning. It is when you take two separate
concepts and find a correlation between the two, thus enhancing your experience. This level of
learning will take place towards the end of your training, once you understand most major
concepts and can apply them.
By understanding which level of learning you’re working towards, you can most effectively plan your
progress forward. If you have trouble determining what level of learning to work on, ask your assigned
instructor!
Again, we welcome you to the program and we are excited to assist you in your journey in becoming a
pilot. Here at Elite Aviation, we pride ourselves on our quality, expertise, and customer experience. If
you have any questions at all we would be pleased to help you.
Congratulations in getting started,

Private Pilot Maneuvers
Cessna 172-S
TAKEOFF:
Normal Takeoff
• Complete the Before Takeoff Checklist and review the Takeoff Checklist
• Turn applicable lights on, set transponder to ALT mode, check wind indicator, and
ensure clearance (if applicable) prior to taxiing onto the active runway.
• When aligned with runway centerline, verify heading indicator, note time, smoothly
add full power
• Check tachometer to verify full power is being developed by the engine, airspeed
indicator is alive, and engine instruments are in the green
• Rotate at 55 KIAS (Vr)
• Pitch to establish 74 KIAS (Vy) after leaving the runway surface
Cross Wind Takeoff
• Complete the Before Takeoff Checklist and review the Takeoff Checklist
• Turn applicable lights on, set transponder to ALT mode, and check wind indicator, and
ensure clearance (if applicable) prior to taxiing onto the active runway.
• When aligned with runway centerline, turn ailerons fully into the wind, verify heading
indicator, note time, and smoothly add full power
• Check tachometer to verify full power is being developed by the engine, airspeed
indicator is alive, and engine instruments are in the green
• During takeoff roll, reduce aileron deflection as needed to maintain runway centerline
• Rotate at 55 KIAS (Vr)
• Pitch to establish 74 KIAS (Vy) after leaving runway surface
• Maintain the extended runway centerline through an appropriate crab angle, or
sideslip
Short Field Takeoff
• Complete the Before Takeoff Checklist and review the Short Field Takeoff Checklist
• Deploy 10° of flaps, turn applicable lights on, set transponder to ALT mode, and check
wind indicator, and ensure clearance (if applicable) prior to taxiing onto the active
runway.
• Taxi to the runway end using all available pavement
• Align yourself with the runway centerline, moving forward just enough to allow the
nose wheel to straighten and apply full brakes
• Verify heading indicator, note time, and smoothly apply full power
• Check tachometer to verify full power is being developed by the engine, and engine
instruments are in the green
• Release the brakes
• Verify airspeed indicator is alive
• Rotate at 50 KIAS and then pitch to establish 56 KIAS (Vx)
• Once at 50’ AGL, or when advised, and when all obstacles are cleared, pitch to
establish a climb at 74 KIAS (Vy)
• Above 60 KIAS retract the flaps
Soft Field Takeoff
• Complete the Before Takeoff Checklist and review applicable items from the Takeoff
Checklist
• Deploy 10° of flaps, Turn applicable lights on, set transponder to ALT mode, and check
wind indicator, and ensure clearance (if applicable) prior to taxiing onto the active
runway.
• Taxi onto the runway with full aft elevator pressure and align on runway centerline
without coming to a full stop
• Smoothly apply full power, once the nose wheel is off the runway surface, begin to
reduce backpressure to maintain forward visibility while keeping the nose wheel off
the runway
• Check tachometer to verify full power is being developed by the engine, airspeed
indicator is alive, and engine instruments are in the green
• Once airborne, level off in ground effect until the aircraft reaches 74 KIAS (Vy) (Or Vx
if appropriate), then climb out maintaining that speed (Vy or Vx as appropriate)
• Above 100’ AGL (or all obstacles clear) and above 60 KIAS and positive rate of climb,
retract the flaps
LANDINGS:
Normal Landing
• Complete the Before Landing and Landing Checklists
• Descend to pattern altitude ~3 miles prior to entering traffic pattern, or as appropriate
• At midfield downwind perform the CGUMPS procedure:
o C – Clearance: permission to land/option from ATC if applicable
o G – Gas: Fuel selector valve on both
o U – Undercarriage: Verify gear down (or fixed gear)
o M – Mixture: Full rich
o P – Power: reduce to establish 100 KIAS
o S – Seatbelts: latched and locked in place for all occupants
• Abeam the touch down point, reduce power to 1500 RPM, or as appropriate
• Deploy 10° of flaps when at or below 110 (Vfe)
• Pitch to establish descent at 80 KIAS and trim as necessary
• On base, deploy flaps to 20° (as appropriate) ensuring under 85 KIAS, pitch to
establish 75 KIAS and adjust trim as necessary
• On final, re-verify wind conditions, maintain runway centerline, deploy flaps to 30° (as
appropriate), pitch to establish 65 KIAS (+ ½ the gust factor) and trim as necessary
• Reduce power as needed, and reduce to idle when runway is assured
Cross Wind Landing
• Complete the Before Landing and Landing Checklists
• Descend to pattern altitude ~3 miles prior to entering traffic pattern, or as appropriate
• At midfield downwind perform the CGUMPS procedure:
o C – Clearance: permission to land/option from ATC if applicable
o G – Gas: Fuel selector valve on both
o U – Undercarriage: Verify gear down (or fixed gear)
o M – Mixture: Full rich
o P – Power: reduce to establish 100 KIAS
o S – Seatbelts: latched and locked in place for all occupants
• Abeam the touch down point, reduce power to 1500 RPM, or as appropriate
• Deploy 10° of flaps when at or below 110 (Vfe)
• Pitch to establish descent at 80 KIAS and trim as necessary
• On base, deploy flaps to 20° (as appropriate) ensuring under 85 KIAS, pitch to
establish 75 KIAS and adjust trim as necessary
• On final, re-verify wind conditions, establish sideslip by applying ailerons into the wind
as needed and opposite rudder as needed to maintain runway centerline
• Pitch to establish 65 KIAS (+ ½ the gust factor) and trim as necessary
• Reduce the power as needed, and reduce the power to idle when runway is assured,
while maintaining the sideslip and centerline
• Touch down on the upwind main gear first, followed by the opposite main gear, and
then the nose gear
• After touchdown, gradually turn ailerons fully into the wind
Short Field Landing
• Complete the Before Landing and Short Field Landing Checklists
• Descend to pattern altitude ~3 miles prior to entering traffic pattern, or as appropriate
• At midfield downwind perform the CGUMPS procedure:
o C – Clearance: permission to land/option from ATC if applicable
o G – Gas: Fuel selector valve on both
o U – Undercarriage: Verify gear down (or fixed gear)
o M – Mixture: Full rich
o P – Power: reduce to establish 100 KIAS
o S – Seatbelts: latched and locked in place for all occupants
• Select and announce the desired touch down point
• When abeam selected touch down point reduce power to 1500 RPM, as appropriate
• Deploy 10° of flaps when at or below 110 (Vfe)
• Pitch to establish descent at 80 KIAS and trim as necessary
• On base, deploy flaps to 20° (as appropriate) ensuring under 85 KIAS, pitch to
establish 75 KIAS and adjust trim as necessary
• On final, re-verify the wind conditions, maintain runway centerline, deploy flaps to
30° (as appropriate), pitch to establish 61 KIAS and trim as necessary
• Adjust power as needed and reduce power to idle as appropriate to land on or shortly
after the identified touch down point
• Upon touch down, apply smooth and equal braking without locking the brakes, add
backpressure, and retract the flaps for maximum brake effectiveness
Soft Field Landing
• Complete the Before Landing and Short Field Landing Checklists
• Descend to pattern altitude ~3 miles prior to entering traffic pattern, or as appropriate
• At midfield downwind perform the CGUMPS procedure:
o C – Clearance: permission to land/option from ATC if applicable
o G – Gas: Fuel selector valve on both
o U – Undercarriage: Verify gear down (or fixed gear)
o M – Mixture: Full rich
o P – Power: reduce to establish 100 KIAS
o S – Seatbelts: latched and locked in place for all occupants
• Abeam the touch down point, reduce power to 1500-1700 RPM as appropriate
• Deploy 10° of flaps when at or below 110 (Vfe)
• Pitch to establish descent at 80 KIAS and trim as necessary
• On base, deploy flaps to 20° (as appropriate) ensuring under 85 KIAS and pitch to
establish 75 KIAS and adjust trim as necessary
• On final, re-verify the wind conditions, maintain runway centerline, deploy flaps to
30° (as appropriate), pitch to establish 65 KIAS and trim as necessary
• Reduce the power as needed and when the runway is assured, reduce the power to
just above idle, flare, and land softly;
o OR reduce the power to idle as appropriate and add power as needed during the
flare to control the rate of descent and land softly
• Touch down gently on the main gear while maintaining backpressure to allow the nose
gear to remain off of the runway until it drops naturally, using MINIMAL braking
Power Off 180 Landing (AKA Short Approach) (With instructor only)
• Reduce throttle to idle smoothly when abeam the point of intended landing on downwind
or when instructed
• Pitch to establish glide at 68 KIAS (Vg)
• Trim as necessary
• Turn base at an appropriate distance from the runway based on the strength and
direction of the wind
• Deploy up to 30° of flaps as appropriate only when runway is assured
• On final, re-verify the wind conditions, maintain 68 KIAS (Vg) and trim as necessary
• NEVER attempt to stretch the glide or retract the flaps to reach the point of intended
landing
Go-Around
• Immediately and simultaneously:
o Smoothly apply full power
o Pitch slightly up to achieve and maintain a safe airspeed – above 60 KIAS
o Retract flaps to 20°
• After gaining a positive rate of climb and maintaining a safe airspeed, retract flaps to 10°
• Once clear of obstacles, at a safe altitude, and above 65 KIAS, retract to 0° of flaps and
climb as normal at 74 KIAS (Vy)
• Inform ATC or traffic of go-around
o RECALL: (Aviate, Navigate, Communicate; in that order)
Forward Slips to Land
• Use of flaps during slips are prohibited unless with a CFI, or in the event of an emergency
• Do not induce a skidding turn: Use of slips during turns not recommended
• Reduce throttle appropriately for altitude
• Apply opposing aileron and rudder inputs to maintain track toward centerline, and
forward elevator input to maintain pitch down attitude
• When on appropriate glidepath and landing point is assured, remove or reduce the
opposing control inputs to continue a normal approach to landing or sideslip as
appropriate
MANEUVERS:
Steep Turns
• Maintain an altitude above 1500’ AGL
• Accomplish clearing turns, either one 180° turn or two 90° turns
• Perform the Pre-Maneuver Flow:
o Ignition switch – both position
o Master switch – both position
o Throttle – adjust as appropriate to establish entry airspeed of 95 KIAS
o Mixture – full rich if below 3000’ MSL
o Flaps – clean configuration
o Fuel Selector Valve – both position
• Roll into a 45° bank
• Perform a 360° turn in either direction
• Adjust power as necessary to maintain airspeed
• Adjust backpressure as appropriate to maintain altitude
• Nose up trim as appropriate
• Roll out on entry heading, adjusting power, backpressure, and/or trim as necessary to
maintain altitude and airspeed
• Repeat in the opposite direction of turn
• Maintain coordination throughout the maneuver
Slow Flight
• Maintain an altitude above 1500’ AGL
• Accomplish clearing turns, either one 180° turn or two 90° turns
• Perform the Pre-Maneuver Flow:
o Ignition switch – both position
o Master switch – both position
o Throttle – reduce the power to 1500 – 1700 RPM
o Mixture – full rich if below 3000’ MSL
o Flaps – Deploy 10° of flaps when at or below 110 KIAS (Vfe)
o Fuel Selector Valve – both position
• Deploy the remaining flaps in 10° increments once below 85 KIAS.
• Pitch to establish and maintain desired airspeed
• Adjust power as appropriate to maintain altitude
• Trim as necessary
• Maintain coordination throughout the maneuver
• Recovery:
o Smoothly add full power while maintaining original altitude
o Allow the airspeed to increase
o Retract flaps to 20°
o Above 65 KIAS, retract the remaining flaps in 10° increments
o Above 100 KIAS, return to a cruise flight configuration per Cruise Checklist
Power Off Stall
• Maneuver is to be completed at an altitude above 1500’ AGL
• Accomplish clearing turns, either one 180° turn or two 90° turns
• Perform the Pre-Maneuver Flow:
o Ignition switch – both position
o Master switch – both position
o Throttle – reduce the power to 1500 – 1700 RPM
o Mixture – full rich
o Flaps – Deploy 10° of flaps when at or below 110 KIAS (Vfe)
o Fuel Selector Valve – both position
• Maintain altitude with backpressure and allow the airspeed to fall below 85 KIAS
• Deploy the remaining flaps in 10° increments
• Establish a controlled descent at 65 KIAS for 200’
• Maintain wings level or establish up to a 20° bank as instructed
• Maintain coordination throughout the maneuver
• Reduce the power to idle and add backpressure until the critical angle of attack is reached
• Recognize and announce vocally the horn, buffet, and stall
• Recovery:
o Decrease the angle of attack and smoothly add full power
o Maintain coordination throughout
o Retract the flaps to 20°
o Establish a climb
o When above 65 KIAS, retract flaps to 10°
o When above Vx or Vy as appropriate, retract the last 10° of flaps.
o Upon leveling off on starting or specified altitude, return to cruise flight per Cruise
Checklist

• Perform the Pre-Maneuver Flow:
o Ignition switch – both position
o Master switch – both position

S-Turns
• Maintain an altitude between 600’ and 1000’ AGL
• Find an area that is clear of congestion obstructions and terrain
• Accomplish clearing turns, either one 180° turn or two 90° turns
• Perform the Pre-Maneuver Flow:
o Ignition switch – both position
o Master switch – both position
o Throttle – cruise setting
o Mixture – full rich
o Flaps – clean configuration
o Fuel Selector Valve – both position
• Find a ground reference line (road, railroad, field line, tree line) that runs perpendicular
to the wind
• Recommend entry from downwind
• Cross your reference line with wings level
• Roll into a bank not to exceed 45° and vary bank angle based on groundspeed to create a
perfect semi-circle
• Roll out wings level when crossing reference line
• Repeat the maneuver in the opposite direction of turn to create a second perfect semicircle
EMERGENCY PROCEDURE PRACTICE:
Simulated Engine Failure (With instructor only)
• Smoothly reduce power to idle
• Pitch and trim as necessary to establish 68 KIAS (Vg)
• Identify best field of intended landing
• NOTE: Vocalize but do not perform actions contrary to safety or that will result in the
engine or electrical system shutting off
• Perform Engine Failure During Flight Checklist
• If engine fails to restart, perform the Landing Without Engine Power Checklist
• Transponder – simulate/mention setting to 7700
• Simulate a distress call to ATC on a nearby frequency or on emergency frequency 121.5
(Do not transmit)
• Recovery:
o Between 600-1000’ AGL
o Smoothly add full power
o Pitch to establish 74 KIAS (Vy)
o Retract flaps in 10° increments if they were deployed
o Trim as necessary
Simulated Emergency Descent (With instructor only)
• Smoothly reduce power to idle
• Pitch to establish airspeed at 100 KIAS (for training purposes)
• Establish up to 45° of bank as instructed while in a descent
• Trim as appropriate
• Recovery:
o Establish wings level if in a descending turn
o Smoothly increase backpressure to establish a level flight attitude
o Smoothly increase power as necessary
o Fully recover at an altitude above 1000’ AGL

Checkride Guide:

• Dual instruction entries must include instructor name, certificate number, and expiration date
• Log each leg of multi leg flights on separate lines
